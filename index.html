About MDCC

The mission of a college cultural club focused on Dogri language is to promote and preserve the rich cultural heritage of Dogri-speaking regions. Through various activities such as language workshops, cultural performances, and awareness campaigns, the club aims to foster a deeper appreciation for Dogri language, literature, and traditions among students and the broader community. It also seeks to create a platform for dialogue and celebration of Dogri culture, strengthening cultural identity and unity.

Where did the word "Dogri" came from?


--THEORIES--

Intellectuals in the court of Maharaja Ranbir Singh, son of Maharaja Gulab Singh of Jammu and Kashmir described Duggar as a distorted form of the word Dwigarta, which means 'two troughs', a possible reference to the Mansar and Surinsar lakes. It is believed that son of Arjun shot an illustrious arrow on the ground of Mansar and a spring gushed from the land and became Surinsar Lake.

The linguist George Grierson connected the term Duggar with the Rajasthani word Doonger which means 'hill', and Dogra with Donger. This opinion has lacked support because of the inconsistency of the ostensible changes from Rajasthani to Dogri, and has been contradicted by some scholars.

Yet another proposal stems from the word Durgara, the name of a kingdom mentioned in an eleventh century copper-plate inscription in the Bhuri Singh Museum in Chamba, Himachal Pradesh. The word Durgara means 'invincible' in several Northern Indo-Aryan languages.

In 1976, experts at the All India Oriental Conference in Dharwad couldn't agree on Dwigarta and Durgara but found a Doonger-Duggar connection. By 1982 in Jaipur, linguists noted similarities between Rajasthan and Duggar, suggesting a link between Duggar, Dogra, and areas with many forts in both regions.

What is a "Lok Geet"?

Lok Geet is a term used to describe folk songs written and composed by the local people. It is not created by a single person, but rather by the entire local community. Lok Geet has been a part of Indian society since ancient times and is still popular today. The term is often used to describe songs that are written for and by the local people. Lok Geet is often composed in a simple and easy-to-understand style, without worrying about the rules of classical music.
